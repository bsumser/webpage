Power-Series

Power series take the form of:

$∑n=0^∞{an(x-c)n}=a0+a1(x-c)+a2(x-c)2+…$

\(\sum_{n=0}^\infty{a_n(x-2)^n}\)

This is a power series centered at \(c=2\).

\(\sum_{n=0}^\infty{a_n(x+4)^n}\)

This is a power series centered at \(c=-4\). \(x+4=0\) \(x=-4\) \(c=-4\)

\(\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{(x^n)}{n!}\)

This power series is centered at \(c=0\). It can also be written as: \((x-0)^n\)

\(\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{(3x-2)^n}{n}\)

\(3x-2=0\)

\(3x=2\)

\(x=\frac{2}{3}\)

When the the \(\lim_{n \to \infty}=0\), the radius of convergence is \(\infty\), and the interval of convergence is \((-\infty,\infty)\).

When the the \(\lim_{n \to \infty}=\infty\), the radius of convergence is \(0\), and the interval of convergence is \((0,0)\) (interval is centered at 0).
